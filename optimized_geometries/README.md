The potential energy surface of HBQ was generated along the proton-transfer coordinate by propagating
the converged SA-CASSCF wave function sequentially: the converged solution for the ith geometry was 
used as the initial guess for geometry i+1 across the full pathway, ensuring continuous and smooth 
energy profiles. The underlying pathway geometries were obtained from a nudged elastic band (NEB) 
calculation and subsequently optimized with L-PDFT on both S0 and S1, while enforcing bond-distance 
constraints on the transferring O-H and N-H bonds. Energies are reported in Hartree (Eh), the proton 
transfer coordinate and all Cartesian coordinates are given in Angstrom.
